# Ad copy best practices (for generating video-ad copy)

> **Start from what already works:** before writing, read the account's proven-winner swipe
> file at `knowledge/accounts/<account>/winning_copy.md` (generated by `copy-library` — top ROAS
> ads with their actual primary text / headline / description). Use the patterns that win on this
> account as your base, then adapt to the new creative.

The agent reads the creative brief (transcript + frames) plus this guide and the account
profile, then drafts **5 primary texts, 5 headlines, and 5 descriptions**.

## Standard practice (applies to every ad)

- **Ship all options in ONE ad.** Put up to **5 primary texts + 5 headlines + 5 descriptions**
  in a single ad (Meta's multiple-text-options via `asset_feed_spec`) and let Meta optimize which
  combination shows. This is operator-written copy we control — it is NOT "Advantage+ creative
  enhancements" (image expansion / music / auto-rewrites), which stay **off** per account policy.
- **Then prune.** After the ad runs ~1 week, follow up on which text is winning and trim the ad
  down to the best-performing option(s). Schedule that follow-up when the ad goes live.
- **Capitalization rule:** **"Elder" and "Sister" are ALWAYS capitalized** (missionary titles) —
  in every primary text, headline, and description. Keep copy grounded
in what the video actually says/shows — never invent product claims not supported by the brief.

## The three fields
- **Primary text** (the main caption): lead with a hook in the **first ~125 characters** (the
  rest is hidden behind "…more"). Benefit-first, conversational, 1–3 short lines. End with a
  clear next step. Emojis sparingly (0–2). No walls of text.
- **Headline** (~27–40 chars, shown under the video): the offer or core benefit, punchy.
- **Description** (link description, optional): a short supporting line — shipping, guarantee,
  range, social proof.

## Write 5 *distinct* angles (don't paraphrase one idea five times)
1. **Benefit-led** — the main outcome the customer gets.
2. **Problem → solution** — the pain the product removes.
3. **Social proof** — reviews, popularity, "loved by…".
4. **Offer / urgency** — promo, limited run, seasonal (only if real).
5. **Story / identity** — who it's for, the feeling/vibe from the video.

## Match the creative
- Use the transcript's actual hook/words; mirror the video's tone (the "vibe").
- If frames suggest a style (minimal, bold, lifestyle), reflect it in the copy.
- Pick a **call-to-action** that fits the destination (Shop Now, Learn More, etc.).

## Compliance / quality guardrails
- No sensational or unverifiable claims; no "you"-based personal-attribute callouts
  (health, finances, age) — Meta rejects these.
- Avoid ALL-CAPS shouting, excessive punctuation, or misleading clickbait.
- Match the landing page; don't promise what the page doesn't deliver.

## For this account (Divine Designs — jewelry)
Goal is **3.0 ROAS**; optimize for purchases. Quality, handmade/meaningful angle tends to fit;
keep it tasteful, benefit- and gift-oriented. (See `accounts/divine_designs/profile.md` for
audiences and current performance.)
